$$tan\alpha=\dfrac{sin\alpha}{cos\alpha}$$ $$sin(2\alpha)=2*cos\alpha*sin\alpha$$ $$sin(3\alpha)=3*sin\alpha-4*sin^3\alpha$$ $$sin^2\alpha=\dfrac{1-cos(2\alpha)}{2}$$
$$cot\alpha=\dfrac{cos\alpha}{sin\alpha}$$ $$sin(2\alpha)=\dfrac{2*tan\alpha}{1+tan^2\alpha}$$ $$cos(3\alpha)=4*cos^3\alpha-3*cos\alpha$$ $$cos^2\alpha=\dfrac{1cos(2\alpha)}{2}$$
$$sin^2\alpha+cos^2\alpha=1$$ $$cos(2\alpha)=cos^2\alpha-sin^2\alpha$$ $$tan(3\alpha)=\dfrac{3*tan\alpha-tan^3\alpha}{1-3*tan^2\alpha}$$ $$tan^2\alpha=\dfrac{1-cos(2\alpha)}{1+cos(2\alpha)}$$
$$1+tan^2\alpha=\dfrac{1}{cos^2\alpha}$$ $$cos(2\alpha)=\dfrac{1-tan^2\alpha}{1+tan^2\alpha}$$ $$cot(3\alpha)=\dfrac{cot^3\alpha-3cot\alpha}{3*cot^2\alpha-1}$$ $$cot^2\alpha=\dfrac{1+cos(2\alpha)}{1-cos(2\alpha)}$$
$$1+cot^2\alpha=\dfrac{1}{sin^2\alpha}$$ $$tan(2\alpha)=\dfrac{2*tan\alpha}{1-tan^2\alpha}$$ $$(sin\alpha-cos\alpha)^2=1-sin(2\alpha)$$
$$tan\alpha*cot\alpha=1$$ $$cot(2\alpha)=\dfrac{cot^2\alpha-1}{2*cot\alpha}$$ $$(sin\alpha+cos\alpha)^2=1+sin(2\alpha)$$

Probleme rezolvate

Problema 1.

$$ \begin{align} \text{ Daca }sin\alpha=\frac{3}{4} \text{ si } \alpha \in \begin{pmatrix} 0, \dfrac{\pi}{2} \end{pmatrix} \text{atunci sa se calculeze} (sin(2\alpha)+cos(2\alpha)) \text{.} \end{align} $$

Formule:

$$sin(2\alpha)=2*cos\alpha*sin\alpha$$ $$cos(2\alpha)=cos^2\alpha-sin^2\alpha=2cos^2\alpha-1=1-2sin^2\alpha$$

Rezolvare:

$$sin^2\alpha+cos^2\alpha=1=>cos^2\alpha=1-sin^2\alpha$$

$$cos^2\alpha=1-(\dfrac{3}{4})^2=1-\dfrac{9}{16}=\dfrac{7}{16}$$

$$cos\alpha=\sqrt{\dfrac{7}{16}}=\dfrac{\sqrt{7}}{4}$$

$$cos\alpha=\dfrac{\sqrt{7}}{4}$$ $$sin\alpha=\dfrac{3}{4}$$ $$sin(2\alpha)=2sin\alpha*cos\alpha=3*\dfrac{\sqrt{7}}{8}$$ $$cos(2\alpha)=1-2*sin^2\alpha=1-2*\dfrac{9}{16}=\dfrac{1}{8}$$ $$sin(2\alpha)+cos(2\alpha)=\dfrac{3*\sqrt{7}+1}{8}$$

Problema 2.

$$ \begin{align} \text{Se stie ca } cos\alpha=\dfrac{4}{5} \alpha\in \begin{pmatrix} 0, \dfrac{\pi}{2} \\ \end{pmatrix} \text{ , sa se calculeze } \dfrac{sin(3\alpha}{cos(2\alpha)} \text{.} \end{align} $$

Formule:

$$sin^2\alpha+cos^2\alpha=1$$ $$cos(2\alpha)=cos^2\alpha-sin^2\alpha=2cos^2\alpha-1=1-2sin^2\alpha$$ $$sin(3\alpha)=3*sin\alpha-4*sin^3\alpha$$

Rezolvare:

$$sin^(\alpha)=1-cos^2\alpha=1-\dfrac{16}{25}=\dfrac{9}{25}$$ $$sin\alpha=\sqrt{\dfrac{9}{25}}=\dfrac{3}{5}$$ $$cos(2\alpha)=2*cos^2\alpha-1=2*\dfrac{16}{25}-1=\dfrac{7}{25}$$ $$sin(3\alpha)=3*sin\alpha-4*sin^3\alpha=3*\dfrac{3}{5}-4*\dfrac{108}{125}$$ $$sin(3\alpha)=\dfrac{117}{125}$$ $$\dfrac{sin(3\alpha}{cos(2\alpha)}=\dfrac{117}{125}*\dfrac{25}{7}$$ $$\dfrac{sin(3\alpha}{cos(2\alpha)}=\dfrac{117}{35}$$
